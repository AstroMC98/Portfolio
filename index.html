About me

Researcher turned
production AI engineer

I started doing AI and data science research during my undergraduate years at UP Diliman in 2018, then continued as a research associate at the Philippine Genome Center, co-authoring papers on SARS-CoV-2 genomic surveillance and building data pipelines for national genomic analysis. That foundation in rigorous, reproducible science shaped how I approach engineering problems today.

From there I moved into industry — data science at AIA (Asia-Pacific's largest insurance group), then managing AI initiatives at TDCX AI in Singapore, and now leading enterprise LLM development at Intelligent AI Solutions, a UK-based consultancy. Along the way I've won 4 national and international hackathons, trained corporate and government teams on AI, and consulted for a Philippine Science High School on data-driven student outcomes.

I'm most energised by the gap between "it works in a notebook" and "it runs reliably at scale" — async pipelines, evaluation frameworks, rate limiting, drift detection, and the architectural decisions that make AI systems maintainable in production.
